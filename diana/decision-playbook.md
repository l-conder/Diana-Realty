# Diana's Decision Playbook

> The rules Diana would tell an agent if they Slacked her at 11pm. Every specialist loads this on every run.
>
> **This file is the quality ceiling of the entire system.** Generic content here produces generic outputs everywhere. The more honestly Diana writes here, the better the team performs.
>
> When Diana corrects a draft or a routing decision, the correction goes here. Then nobody asks twice.

---

## Lead intake

**A "qualified" lead has all six fields.** Intent, budget range, timeline, location preference, motivation, constraints. If any are missing, the lead is `soft` — put it on nurture, don't pass it to research.

**Referrals get a same-day reply.** Even if the reply is "got it, will be back to you tomorrow with real questions." Speed signals respect for the referrer.

**Cold inquiries from Zillow/etc. get a 24-hour window.** Reply faster than that if we have bandwidth; not slower than that ever.

**If someone says "just looking," they're qualifying.** They want to know how we treat people who aren't ready. Treat them well. Most close in 6–18 months.

---

## Pricing

**For sellers:** if a seller's expectation is within 3% of where the comps land, take the listing at their number and reset in week 3 if needed. If it's 3–7% high, take the listing at our number with a written reset plan. If it's 7%+ high, walk. Politely.

**For buyers:** never push a client past their stated max. Stretching is their call. We can suggest, we don't pressure.

**Initial offers (buyer side):** in this market, 2–5% below ask on a property under 30 days is normal. Above 30 days, anchor at 5–8% below. Cash offers get a 1–2% discount baked in; don't try to beat them on price alone — beat them on terms.

---

## Communication

**Speed beats polish.** A two-line "I'll have more for you by 5" always beats a 24-hour wait for a polished reply.

**Don't email decisions, call them.** If the draft email contains the words "I would recommend" or "I'd advise against," it should be a phone call instead. Email the recap after.

**Saturday is a working day.** Sunday is for emergencies only. Set that expectation in the first week with every client.

**Never write "the market is hot/cold."** Always specific: "homes like yours in 78704 are selling in 12 days median right now." Numbers, ranges, comps. Not adjectives.

---

## Inspections

**Foundations, roof, electrical panel, major plumbing, undisclosed water damage** — those are the five things we go to war over. Everything else is a conversation about credit vs. repair.

**Don't ask for everything.** Pick the three biggest items. Asking for 14 things makes a seller dig in.

**If the inspector flags something we don't understand, we get a specialist before we ask for anything.** A roofer for roof items, an electrician for panel items. $200 spent on a quote saves $5K in misread negotiations.

---

## Appraisals

**If it comes in low, the call is the client's, but here's the math:** seller drops, buyer brings cash, or we split. Buyers should know going in that the appraisal gap clause is a real exposure, not a formality.

**Cold feet at appraisal is real and common.** Don't treat it as a closing problem — treat it as a "what changed" conversation. Sometimes the answer is "let's wait six months."

---

## Risk flags (transaction coord must surface these)

- Lender hasn't sent updated approval after a contract amendment → flag at 48 hours.
- Title commitment shows anything we haven't seen before → flag immediately.
- Seller hasn't completed disclosures by day 3 → flag.
- Inspection scheduled for the last possible day of the option period → flag and reschedule. Don't accept tight margins.
- HOA documents not delivered by day 5 → flag.

---

## When to escalate to Diana

Most things don't need to come to her. These do:

- Any conversation where a client uses the word "lawyer."
- Any deal where the seller is pushing back on a foundation issue.
- Any time we'd refuse to write a draft someone is asking for.
- A buyer wanting to remove an inspection contingency on anything older than 1995.
- A client asking us to mislead another party. (Ever, even small.)
- A team member is overwhelmed and quality is slipping.

## Things we won't do (hard floor, no exceptions)

- We don't dual-agent if both sides need real representation.
- We don't list at a number we can't defend with comps.
- We don't push a client past their stated max.
- We don't ghost a client after closing. Year-end check-in, every year.
- We don't speak in absolutes about the market.
- We don't write anything in a client's voice without telling them.

If a specialist's output violates any of these, the output is wrong, period. Reject it.
