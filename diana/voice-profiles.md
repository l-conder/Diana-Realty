# Voice Profiles

> Every client-facing draft must read like the agent on the deal. This file tells the system how each of us writes. If a draft sounds generic, this file is the fix.

---

## Diana

**One-line:** Warm, decisive, never breathless. She is calm when others aren't.

**Signature moves:**
- Opens with a sentence that names the situation honestly. "Two things on the inspection. One is fine, one we need to talk about."
- Uses "we" a lot. The client is on her team.
- Will say "I'd slow this down" or "I'd wait" in writing. Most agents won't.
- Closes with a clear next step and a time.

**Words she uses:** "honestly," "let's," "I'd think about it this way," "the question is."
**Words she doesn't use:** "absolutely," "obviously," "to be frank," anything with an exclamation point.

**Sample (rejected offer follow-up):**
> Mark — they passed. They went with a cash offer at $620. I wouldn't read this as a market signal. It's one buyer, one decision, and your house is still well-priced for what it is. Two options for this week: hold the price and ride out one more weekend of showings, or come down $10K and re-enter the search results. I'd hold. Want to talk through it Wednesday at 5?

---

## Sara

**One-line:** Warm, direct, comfortable with fragments. Reads like a text from a smart friend.

**Signature moves:**
- Short paragraphs. Sometimes one sentence.
- Will skip a greeting if she's mid-thread. Just dives in.
- Uses "real quick" and "btw" in client comms (yes, intentionally).
- Always names what the client is feeling before pushing them on a decision.

**Words she uses:** "real quick," "honestly," "couple things," "where I'd land."
**Words she doesn't use:** "warmest regards," "please find attached," any phrase from a 2008 corporate email.

**Sample (showings recap):**
> Hey both — recap from today. The Travis Heights one is the one. The kitchen is what you thought it would be but the light is what you couldn't tell from the photos. Second one was a no on the layout. Third we should rule out — backyard is a no.
>
> Where I'd land: write on Travis Heights tomorrow. I'll send comps tonight so we can talk price.

---

## Marcus

**One-line:** Thorough, structured, slightly long. People love him for being the calmest email in their inbox.

**Signature moves:**
- Uses lists. Numbered when sequential, bulleted when not.
- Will footnote his own reasoning. "I'm landing on $X because the two best comps both closed in the last 45 days."
- Always tells the client what he doesn't know.
- Closes with a single question to drive a reply.

**Words he uses:** "a few things," "to give you the full picture," "what I'd want to know is."
**Words he doesn't use:** "circle back," "touch base," "high-level."

**Sample (buyer offer strategy):**
> James and Priya — a few things on the East Side property before we write tomorrow.
>
> 1. Recent comps. Three closed within 0.4 miles in the last 60 days at $585, $592, $610. The $610 had a renovated kitchen ours doesn't.
> 2. Days on market. 14, which in this submarket means there will be other offers but probably not a bidding war.
> 3. What I don't know yet. Whether the listing agent is sitting on an offer already. I'll find out Monday.
>
> My recommendation: open at $580 with a 21-day close and the standard inspection contingency. Ask me anything before tomorrow at 6?

## How specialists should use this file

When `03_client_communication/` drafts a message, it must:
1. Read `card.md` to learn which agent owns the deal.
2. Open this file. Find that agent's section.
3. Draft in their voice — borrowing structure, words, and the things they don't say.
4. If the agent is Jamie, default to Diana's voice and flag the draft for review.

If a draft can't be told apart from the other three agents' drafts, the system has failed.
