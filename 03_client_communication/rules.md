# Rules — Client Communication

## Always do

- **Always read `_diana/voice-profiles.md` for the assigned agent's voice before drafting.** This is non-negotiable. Marcus's drafts read different from Sara's drafts, period.
- **Always read the case `card.md` and `log.md` before drafting.** The draft should reflect what the team already knows. No re-asking, no contradictions.
- **Always read `_diana/decision-playbook.md` for situation conventions.** "Don't email decisions, call them." "Saturday is a working day, Sunday is for emergencies." "Pick three inspection items." These are not optional.
- **Always lead with the situation, not pleasantries.** No "I hope this finds you well." Open with the thing that matters.
- **Always close with a specific next step and a time.** "Talk Wednesday at 5?" beats "let me know when works."
- **Always default to brevity.** A two-line text or three-paragraph email beats a polished essay 90% of the time.
- **Always flag drafts for Jamie for Diana's review** until Jamie's voice profile is finalized.
- **Always log the draft in the case log,** with the draft itself in a code block (or as a separate `draft_YYYY-MM-DD_subject.md` if longer than a few paragraphs).

## Never do

- **Never use "AI-tell" language:** "exquisite," "boasting," "nestled," "in today's competitive market," "the perfect blend of," "warmest regards," "circle back," "touch base."
- **Never write decisions in email that should be a call.** If the draft contains "I would recommend" or "I'd advise against" or "I'd be worried about," it should be a phone call. Draft the call-prep notes instead.
- **Never claim absolutes about the market.** Numbers, ranges, comps — yes. "It's a great time to sell" — no.
- **Never push a client past their stated budget or timeline.** Suggest, don't pressure.
- **Never send a draft that violates Diana's hard floor** (per `_diana/about-diana.md` and `decision-playbook.md`). If you've drafted something the team won't do, the draft is wrong.
- **Never write in a client's voice without their knowledge.** We don't ghostwrite *as* the client.
- **Never send a draft to an outside party.** Drafts go to the agent. The agent sends.

## Situation playbook

The team has a handful of recurring situations. For each, here is the draft skeleton.

### Missed showing
- Acknowledge, don't apologize excessively. (Per voice: brief.)
- Reschedule with two specific options.
- Move on. Don't dwell.

### Competing offers (informing a buyer)
- State the situation plainly: "There's another offer."
- Give the client the math: we know X, we don't know Y.
- Diana's playbook on competing offers: don't try to beat cash on price alone; beat them on terms (option period length, close date, escalation clauses, leaseback if applicable).
- Always frame as a choice. The client makes the call.

### Inspection issues
- Per `decision-playbook.md`: foundation, roof, electrical panel, major plumbing, water damage = war items. Everything else is conversation.
- Pick three items max to negotiate. Asking for everything makes the seller dig in.
- If the buyer is panicking on a non-war item: this is a phone call, not an email.

### Financing delays
- Be straight about what's known, what's not, and the next checkpoint.
- Never speculate about whether the deal will close. Just describe the status.
- If TC has flagged a real risk, include the contingency plan.

### Appraisal gap
- Three options: seller drops, buyer brings cash, split the gap.
- The math goes in the email. The decision is the client's.
- If the buyer is using the gap clause, the email reminds them what they signed up for.

### Cold feet
- Phone call, not email. (Per playbook.)
- The draft is *call-prep notes*, not an email.
- Sometimes the right answer is "let's wait six months." That's a real option.

### Lost-out buyer
- "They went with another offer at $X." State it.
- "I wouldn't read this as a market signal." Frame the loss correctly.
- Two options for next: hold the search, adjust criteria. Client picks.

### Listing presentation cover
- Built from `02_property_research`'s CMA brief.
- In the listing agent's voice (probably Sara, sometimes Marcus, occasionally Diana).
- Pricing recommendation goes in the cover with reasoning. No hand-waving.

### Nurture cadence (soft leads)
- 30 days: a useful market data point relevant to their target area.
- 90 days: a check-in with one specific question.
- 180 days: "still on your timeline?"
- Each touch in the agent's voice. Each one short.

### Year-end check-in (closed clients)
- One paragraph. Specific. References something from their actual transaction.
- No newsletter content. This is a human-to-human note.

## Output format

Every draft goes in the case log. Format:

```
YYYY-MM-DD HH:MM — 03_client_communication (initials) — [draft for situation X, in agent Y's voice]. → next: [agent] to review and send.

---DRAFT---
[subject if email]
[body]
---END DRAFT---
```

For drafts longer than ~200 words or for listing presentation cover notes, save as `draft_YYYY-MM-DD_subject.md` in the case folder and reference it in the log entry.
